Together with two other teammates, I helped design and develop Perceptix—a platform for researchers to create quizzes testing AI awareness. Users sign up with email verification and require
admin approval to become 'researchers,' while others remain 'visitors.' The authentication and authorization processses use JWTs with role info. The quiz editor lets researchers upload stimuli,
select question types (AI detection or identification), and view analytics such as average time, accuracy, partial completions, and sorting. You can also browse quizzes and profiles of
researchers, allowing you to view their public quizzes. Researchers can create a bio and upload a profile image, and it exists a dark mode as well.

My solo contributions included the admin page, tracking page visits, showing analytics for quizzes, designing the landing page, and UX/UI changes in the quiz editor. Working under a tight timeframe taught us time management,
how to perform under pressure, and the importance of following a consistent coding architecture style... the latter we will be better at next time.
